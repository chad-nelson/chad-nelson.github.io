Hotel Markup Automation

This project was to automatically adjust airfare pricing based on data inputs to determine if it was packaged with a hotel purchase. There are offers which discount rates if both airfare and lodging are purchased, so in order to maximize profitability, if airfare was being purchased with lodging, we would adjust the airfare so we would still get our full rate. I was able to take lead on this project because I had a better knowledge of Python than my coworkers and was able to utilize its capabilities more effectively.

Big Data Analytics Project

In this project, I used Spark R in the Databricks analytics platform to predict the payment type of taxi customers based on multiple input variables. The data used is taxi data from New York City during August 2014. I used both a logistic regression and random forest model to predict the outcome of payment type. After viewing the results, it appears that passenger count was the largest indicator of payment type based on the input variables used. There is no surcharge for extra passengers in New York City taxis, but this could be a result of people tipping more when their group is larger. If the customer tips more, they may not have enough cash on hand or not want to use a large amount of their cash.
